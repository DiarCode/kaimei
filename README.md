# Kaimei: A Certified, Multi-Agent Self‑Driving Lab OS for Autonomous Scientific Discovery

The KAIMEI platform represents a fundamental reimagining of scientific discovery, transforming it from a **human-centric, capital-intensive process** into a **software-accessible, autonomous capability**. The core mission centers on three interconnected objectives that distinguish it from existing solutions.

**First, zero-human-iteration autonomous discovery**: Unlike current systems that automate execution but require human guidance at each decision point, KAIMEI closes the entire loop—from hypothesis upload through experimental design, virtual and physical execution, iterative refinement, and publication-grade output—without requiring ongoing human intervention. This is not incremental automation but a **paradigm shift to "Science 3.0"**. 

**Second, democratized access**: While big pharma has deployed self-driving laboratories (A-Lab, DeepMind/Isomorphic), these capabilities remain locked behind **multi-million-dollar capital investments** and proprietary infrastructure. KAIMEI's cloud-native, multi-tenant SaaS architecture enables SMEs, universities, and citizen scientists to access equivalent capabilities through a natural language interface, with tiered pricing (free virtual tier, pay-per-experiment physical execution). 

**Third, trustworthy autonomy**: The platform addresses the **critical failure mode of "confident nonsense"**—AI systems that generate plausible but dangerous or invalid outputs—through machine-verifiable safety guarantees, not merely soft guidelines or human oversight. This is essential for regulatory acceptance and societal license to operate.

The target domains—**drug discovery, materials science, and climate solutions**—were selected for their **societal urgency** and **technical fit**. Drug discovery faces 10-15 year timelines and $2.6B costs per approved therapy; materials science offers breakthrough potential in energy and sustainability; climate solutions demand accelerated innovation cycles that traditional methodologies cannot deliver.
